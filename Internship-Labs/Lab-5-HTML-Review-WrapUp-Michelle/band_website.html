About the Band

Summary

The Jonas Brothers are a group consisting of three brothers and formed a band in 2005. However, they gained more popularity through the Disney Channel when they starred in the movie Camp Rock in 2008.

Early career

The band's first song was recorded in 2005, and had considered the name Sons of Jonas, but ultimately settled on the Jonas Brothers. They were signed by Columbia Records and toured throughout the year with other popular names. The album was finally released in August 2006, and around that same time, the band recorded a theme song for a Disney Channel show, as well as multiple covers that gained popularity on the channel.

A New Label

The band switched labels in 2007 and signed with Hollywood Records. Their Disney careers continued, as they recorded a few commercials and were featured on multiple soundtracks. They went on to release a few more albums under this label, but continued working with Disney over the years, eventually coming out with their own show, Jonas (and a spin off).

Break Up

The band announced in 2013 that they were taking a hiatus, and pursuing individual projects. During this time, some of the members did release a few solo projects, but none gained the popularity that they did as a group.

Return

Finally, in 2019, the band reunited and put out an album titled Happiness Begins, and went on tour. Since then, they have continued their solo projects, but also make music together.
